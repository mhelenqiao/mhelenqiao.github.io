Bio

I am a PhD candidate in the Causal Cognition Lab in the Experimental Psychology Department at University College London (UCL). My research investigates judgment and decision-making, Bayesian and causal reasoning, coherence-based reasoning, and AI alignment, with a particular focus on reasoning in legal contexts. Using cognitive science and experimental methods, I investigate how people and large language models evaluate evidence and reach judgments, and I develop models to capture these processes.

I am always keen to connect, collaborate, and learn. Reach me at helen.qiao.18@ucl.ac.uk if you'd like to talk about legal psychology, AI, philosophy, human cognition, or anything adjacent and beyond.
